Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam  
Dates: 15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015  
Birthplace/Family:
  - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu; youngest of five. His father was a boat-owner and imam; mother a homemaker; he remained lifelong close to his elder siblings and often supported them financially :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

Education:
  - Attended Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram.
  - BSc (Physics), St. Joseph’s College (1954).
  - MEng (Aeronautical Engineering), Madras Institute of Technology (1960) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Early Career & Perseverance:
  - Began at Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd; joined DRDO (1958), then ISRO (1969).
  - Led SLV‑III project; the first launch failed due to an ignored error (satellite fell into Bay of Bengal), but under his leadership team succeeded in 1980 :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.
  - Project Director for missiles (Agni, Prithvi, Akash, Trishul, Nag); led Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998) earning “Missile Man” title :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Personal Traits & Anecdotes:
  - Vegetarian, teetotaler who never owned a TV; daily routine: rise ~6:30 am, sleep by 2 am :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.
  - Loved Tamil poetry, playing veena, Carnatic devotional music. Wrote poetry in Tamil :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.
  - Lived simply; minimal possessions; left no personal wealth :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.
  - Took his team member’s children to a show when the parent couldn’t, and gave a student a golden Ex-President card for formatting impressive slides :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.
  - Upon receiving a child’s sketch, he personally thanked them—demonstrating deep humility and care :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}.

Spiritual & Cultural Philosophy:
  - Practicing Sunni Muslim who engaged in interfaith dialogue, citing respect for all religions as essential for a unified India :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}.
  - Influenced by Pramukh Swami: wrote *Transcendence* after spiritual experiences with him, highlighting compassion & inner peace :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}.

Legacy & Vision:
  - Advocated for “simple living, high thinking”; emphasized education as foundation for national progress :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}.
  - Encouraged youth for skill development and aimed to interact with 100,000 students post-1999 :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}.
